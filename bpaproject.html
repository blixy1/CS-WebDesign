Our mission is to provide students with easy access to mental health information, resources, and encouragement to seek help when they need it.

Why This Matters

1 in 5 teens struggles with mental health challenges. Awareness and early support can make a huge difference. Our website connects students to reliable information and local support options.

Depression

  • What it is: More than occasional sadness clinical depression severely affects thoughts, emotions, and daily life.

  • Symptoms: Persistent sadness, loss of interest, changes in sleep/appetite, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, feelings of guilt or worthlessness.

  • Causes: Genetics, brain chemistry, environmental stress, psychological factors.

  • Impact: Can interfere with work, school, and relationships; may require therapy or medication.

    • Anxiety

    • What it is: Feeling anxious is normal, but anxiety disorders involve persistent, excessive worry that doesn’t go away and can get worse over time.

    • Common triggers: Health, money, school, work, family.

    • Symptoms: Restlessness, rapid heartbeat, trouble concentrating, irritability, sleep problems.

    • Impact: Can affect daily life, relationships, and work/school performance

    Bipolar Disorder 

    • What it is: A mental illness causing extreme mood swings, including high and low periods.

    • Manic episodes: Elevated mood, high energy, reduced need for sleep, impulsive behavior.

    • Depressive episodes: Sadness, low energy, hopelessness, difficulty functioning.

    • Impact: Mood shifts affect relationships, work, and overall functioning.

     Schizophrenia

    • What it is: A serious mental illness that affects thinking, feelings, and behavior; may cause a loss of touch with reality.

    • Symptoms: Hallucinations (hearing voices, seeing things), delusions (false beliefs), disorganized thinking, difficulty functioning socially or at work.

    • Onset: Usually diagnosed between ages 16–30, often after a first psychotic episode.

    • Impact: Can be distressing for the person and their family; requires long-term treatment, often including medication and therapy.

    Helpful Resources

    • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988 (24/7 support)
    • Crisis Text Line: Text HELLO to 741741
    • NAMI HelpLine: 1-800-950-NAMI (6264)
    • Local Counselors: Contact your school’s counseling department or community centers.

    How You Can Help

    • Check in on friends and classmates.
    • Share positive messages about self-care.
    • Report bullying or signs of distress.
    • Encourage open discussions about mental health.

    Self-Care Tips

    • Take breaks and rest your mind.
    • Get enough sleep and eat balanced meals.
    • Stay active with exercise or outdoor time.
    • Talk about your feelings with someone you trust.
